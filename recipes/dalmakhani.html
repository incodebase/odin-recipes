Dal Makhani

Description

The dal makhani is a dish originating in New Delhi,India. It is a modern take on the age-old urad ki dal (black lentil dal also known as Maa ki Dal)

It is made using urad dal (black lentils) and rajma (red kidney beans) that have been soaked overnight. These are mixed with tomato puree, a selection of herbs and spices that include garam masala, sabzi masala, ginger-garlic paste, and a large amount of butter. The mix is then left to cook overnight on a tandoor so the dal can get a distinct smoky flavor. Finally, it is served with a generous garnish of fresh butter.

Ingredients

  • 200 gms soaked black lentil (sabut urad dal)
  • 3 tbsp Garlic water
  • ¼ cup butter
  • water as required
For 1st tempering
  • ½ cup ghee
  • 1 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1 tsp degi red chilli powder
  • 1½ cup tomato puree
  • ¼ cup butter
  • salt to taste
For 2nd tempering
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tsp Kasuri methi
  • ½ tsp degi red chilli powder
  • 2 tbsp water
  • ¼ cup butter
For garnish
  • 2-3 butter cubes
  • ½ tbsp fresh cream

Steps

  1. Wash and soak the urad dal overnight.
  2. In a heavy bottom pot add dal, garlic and water and let it cook for at least 1.5 hours on a low flame.
  3. Once the dal is cooked add butter into it.
  4. For the first tempering, take the ghee and heat it in a pan, then add ginger garlic paste, degi red chilli powder, and fresh tomato puree in it. Sauté till the tomato puree is well cooked.
  5. Now add this tempering to the cooked dal and then mash it.
  6. Add butter and salt to it and cook until well combined.
  7. Now for the second tempering, heat ghee in another pan and add kasuri methi, degi red chilli powder and water to it.
  8. Add this tempering to the dal, add butter to it and give it a boil.
  9. Serve hot in a serving bowl and garnish with cream and butter cubes. You could serve it with either roti or jeera rice.
