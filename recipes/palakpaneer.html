Palak Paneer

Description

The palak paneer is a vegetarian dish, consisting of paneer (a type of cottage cheese) in a thick paste made from puréed spinach, called palak in Hindi, Marathi, Gujarati, and other Indian languages.

The terms palak paneer and saag paneer are sometimes used interchangeably in restaurants in the United States and Canada. However, saag paneer is different from traditional palak paneer in that it contains other green leafy vegetables, such as mustard greens, whereas palak paneer only contains spinach. Dhaba restaurants often specialize in palak paneer.

Ingredients

For the palak puree
  • Two bunches of cleaned palak leaves
  • 1 inch of grated ginger
  • 2-3 garlic pods, roughly chopped
  • 2 green chili, chopped
For the gravy
  • 1 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • ¼ tsp cumin seeds
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • a pinch of asafoetida
  • 2-3 small onion, chopped
  • 2-3 garlic pods, chopped
  • 1 medium-sized Tomato, chopped
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds, roasted and crushed
  • 1/2 tbsp. kasoori methi, roasted and crushed
  • ½ tsp Turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp Red chilli powder
  • 2-3 leaves of spinach, chopped
  • 2 bunches of spinach blanched and pureed
  • ½ cup hot water
  • 250-300 gm of paneer cut into cubes
  • 1 tbsp fresh cream
  • Salt as per taste
For the garnish
  • Julienne ginger
  • Fresh cream

Steps

  1. In a pot blanch the spinach leaves in boiling water for 2-3 minutes. Then remove and transfer it immediately into ice cold water
  2. Now take a blender and make a paste after adding the required ginger and garlic into the blender. Then add the cooked palak and again make a smooth paste
  3. For the gravy heat ghee in a pan and then add bay leaf, cumin seeds, and asafoetida. Stir for a minute until there is no fragrance left.
  4. Add the onion and garlic and saute until it turns translucent.
  5. Add tomatoes and stir till they turn soft. After that, add turmeric, red chilli, kasoori methi, crushed coriander seeds and some coriander powder and mix well. Now add the chopped palak leaves.
  6. Now add hot water and the palak puree which we had prepared before into the gravy, also adding as much salt as you'd like and then give it a nice stir.
  7. Transfer the paneer cubes, sprinkle the garam masala and allow it to cook for another minute
  8. Now garnish with fresh cream and ginger julienne
